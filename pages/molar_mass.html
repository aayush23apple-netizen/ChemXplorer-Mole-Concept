Molar Mass

Definition

Molar mass (M) is the mass of one mole of a substance. It is expressed in grams per mole (g/mol) or kilograms per mole (kg/mol). The molar mass is numerically equal to the atomic or molecular weight of the substance.

Molar Mass Formula: M = m/n
Where m = mass of substance, n = number of moles

Units

The SI unit for molar mass is kg/mol, but in chemistry, g/mol is more commonly used. The molar mass is numerically equal to the relative molecular mass or atomic mass expressed in grams.

Calculation Methods

For Elements

Molar mass of an element = Atomic mass of the element in grams

  • Hydrogen (H): 1.008 g/mol
  • Carbon (C): 12.011 g/mol
  • Oxygen (O): 15.999 g/mol

For Compounds

Molar mass of a compound = Sum of atomic masses of all atoms in the formula

Molar Mass = (Number of atoms of element 1 × Atomic mass of element 1) + (Number of atoms of element 2 × Atomic mass of element 2) + ...

Examples

  • Water (H₂O): (2 × 1.008) + 15.999 = 18.015 g/mol
  • Carbon dioxide (CO₂): 12.011 + (2 × 15.999) = 44.009 g/mol
  • Sodium chloride (NaCl): 22.990 + 35.453 = 58.443 g/mol

Relationship with Mole Concept

Molar mass serves as the conversion factor between mass and moles:

Moles = Mass ÷ Molar Mass

Mass = Moles × Molar Mass

Applications

  • Stoichiometric calculations
  • Determination of empirical and molecular formulas
  • Gas density calculations
  • Solution concentration calculations

Important Notes

  • Molar mass depends on the isotopic composition of the element
  • For ionic compounds, we use formula mass instead of molecular mass
  • Molar mass is temperature-independent for solids and liquids
  • For gases, molar mass relates to density at STP

Molar Mass vs Molecular Mass

Molecular mass is the sum of atomic masses in atomic mass units (u), while molar mass is the same value expressed in grams per mole. They are numerically equal but have different units.
