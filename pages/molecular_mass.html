Molecular Mass

Definition

Molecular mass is the sum of the atomic masses of all atoms in a molecule. It is expressed in atomic mass units (u) or daltons (Da). For compounds, molecular mass is numerically equal to the molar mass but expressed in different units.

Molecular Mass Formula: Sum of atomic masses of all constituent atoms

Calculation Method

Molecular Mass = Σ (number of atoms of each element × atomic mass of each element)

Examples

  • Water (H₂O): (2 × 1.008) + 15.999 = 18.015 u
  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂): 12.011 + (2 × 15.999) = 44.009 u
  • Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆): (6 × 12.011) + (12 × 1.008) + (6 × 15.999) = 180.156 u
  • Sulfuric Acid (H₂SO₄): (2 × 1.008) + 32.065 + (4 × 15.999) = 98.078 u

Relationship to Molar Mass

Molecular mass and molar mass are numerically equal but have different units:

  • Molecular mass: expressed in u (atomic mass units)
  • Molar mass: expressed in g/mol (grams per mole)
Example: Molecular mass of water = 18.015 u
Molar mass of water = 18.015 g/mol

Determination Methods

Molecular mass can be determined through:

  • Chemical formula analysis: Sum of atomic masses
  • Mass spectrometry: Direct measurement of molecular ions
  • Colligative properties: Freezing point depression, boiling point elevation
  • Vapor density method: Comparison with known gas densities
  • Dumas method: For volatile compounds

Applications

  • Calculation of molar masses for stoichiometric calculations
  • Determination of empirical and molecular formulas
  • Gas density calculations
  • Colligative property calculations
  • Mass spectrometry analysis

Important Considerations

  • Use the most abundant isotopes for atomic masses
  • Be careful with polyatomic ions (treat as single units)
  • Round to appropriate significant figures
  • Consider isotopic variations for precise work

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing molecular mass with molar mass
  • Forgetting to multiply by the number of atoms
  • Using wrong atomic masses
  • Not considering water of crystallization in hydrated salts
