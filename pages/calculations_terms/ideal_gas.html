The Ideal Gas Law

The ideal gas law combines Boyle's law, Charles's law, and Avogadro's law into a single equation:

PV = nRT

Where:

  • P = pressure (atm)
  • V = volume (L)
  • n = number of moles
  • R = gas constant (0.0821 L·atm·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹)
  • T = temperature (K)

How to Use

Enter known values and leave the unknown variable blank. The calculator will solve for the missing variable.

Note: Temperature must be in Kelvin (K). Convert Celsius to Kelvin by adding 273.15.

Ideal Gas Law Calculator

Examples

  • Example 1: Calculate volume of 2 moles of gas at 298K and 1 atm
    V = (2 × 0.0821 × 298) ÷ 1 = 48.9 L
  • Example 2: Calculate moles in 10L at 2 atm and 273K
    n = (2 × 10) ÷ (0.0821 × 273) = 0.89 moles
  • Example 3: Calculate temperature of 1 mole gas at 5L and 3 atm
    T = (3 × 5) ÷ (1 × 0.0821) = 183 K

Common Conversions

  • Temperature: °C + 273.15 = K
  • Pressure: 1 atm = 760 mmHg = 101.325 kPa
  • Volume: 1 L = 1000 mL = 0.001 m³

Limitations

The ideal gas law is most accurate at:

  • High temperatures
  • Low pressures
  • Non-polar molecules
  • Point masses (no volume)
