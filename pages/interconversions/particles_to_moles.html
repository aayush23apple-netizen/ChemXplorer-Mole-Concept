Particles to Moles Calculator

This calculator converts number of particles to moles using Avogadro's number: moles = particles ÷ 6.022 × 10²³

How it works:

  1. Enter the number of particles (atoms, molecules, or ions)
  2. The calculator divides by Avogadro's number
  3. Result shows the number of moles
moles = Number of particles ÷ N_A
N_A = 6.022 × 10²³ mol⁻¹

Calculate Moles from Particles

Examples

  • Example 1: Calculate moles in 6.022 × 10²³ water molecules
    moles = 6.022 × 10²³ ÷ 6.022 × 10²³ = 1 mole
  • Example 2: Calculate moles in 3.011 × 10²³ carbon atoms
    moles = 3.011 × 10²³ ÷ 6.022 × 10²³ = 0.5 moles
  • Example 3: Calculate moles in 1.204 × 10²⁴ Na⁺ ions
    moles = 1.204 × 10²⁴ ÷ 6.022 × 10²³ = 2 moles

Tips

  • Avogadro's number of particles = 1 mole
  • Use scientific notation for large numbers
  • Be careful with the type of particles (atoms, molecules, ions)
  • For ionic compounds, count total ions of all types
