Interconversions

Interconversions involve converting between different units of measurement in chemistry. These calculators help you convert between mass, moles, particles, volume, and molarity using the mole concept relationships.

Key Relationships:
• 1 mole = 6.022 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro's number)
• 1 mole of gas = 22.4 L at STP
• Moles = Mass ÷ Molar Mass
• Molarity = Moles ÷ Volume (in L)

How to Use the Calculators

  1. Select the appropriate calculator based on what you want to convert
  2. Enter the known values in the input fields
  3. Click the "Calculate" button to get the result
  4. Results are displayed with appropriate units

Important Notes

  • All volume conversions assume STP conditions (0°C, 1 atm) unless otherwise specified
  • Use atomic/molecular masses from the periodic table for calculations
  • Be careful with units - convert to consistent units before calculating
  • For ionic compounds, use formula mass instead of molecular mass

Common Conversion Factors

Conversion Factor Example
Mass to Moles moles = mass ÷ molar mass 18g H₂O = 1 mole H₂O
Moles to Particles particles = moles × 6.022×10²³ 1 mole = 6.022×10²³ particles
Moles to Volume volume = moles × 22.4 L 1 mole gas = 22.4 L
Moles to Molarity M = moles ÷ volume (L) 1 mole in 1L = 1M
