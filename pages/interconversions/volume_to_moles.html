Volume to Moles Calculator

This calculator converts volume of gas at STP to moles using the molar volume: moles = volume ÷ 22.4 L

How it works:

  1. Enter the volume of gas in liters
  2. The calculator divides by the molar volume at STP
  3. Result shows the number of moles
moles = Volume (L) ÷ 22.4 L/mol
(at STP: 0°C and 1 atm)

Calculate Moles from Volume

Examples

  • Example 1: Calculate moles in 22.4 L of any gas at STP
    moles = 22.4 ÷ 22.4 = 1 mole
  • Example 2: Calculate moles in 11.2 L of O₂ at STP
    moles = 11.2 ÷ 22.4 = 0.5 moles
  • Example 3: Calculate moles in 44.8 L of CO₂ at STP
    moles = 44.8 ÷ 22.4 = 2 moles

Important Notes

  • This applies only to gases at STP conditions
  • STP: Standard Temperature (0°C) and Pressure (1 atm)
  • 22.4 L is the molar volume for ideal gases at STP
  • Not applicable to liquids or solids
  • Real gases may deviate slightly from ideal behavior
