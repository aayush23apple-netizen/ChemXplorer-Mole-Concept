Moles to Particles Calculator

This calculator converts moles to number of particles using Avogadro's number: particles = moles × 6.022 × 10²³

How it works:

  1. Enter the number of moles
  2. The calculator multiplies by Avogadro's number
  3. Result shows the number of particles (atoms, molecules, or ions)
Number of particles = moles × N_A
N_A = 6.022 × 10²³ mol⁻¹

Calculate Particles from Moles

Examples

  • Example 1: Calculate particles in 1 mole of water
    particles = 1 × 6.022 × 10²³ = 6.022 × 10²³ molecules
  • Example 2: Calculate atoms in 0.5 moles of carbon
    atoms = 0.5 × 6.022 × 10²³ = 3.011 × 10²³ atoms
  • Example 3: Calculate ions in 2 moles of Na⁺
    ions = 2 × 6.022 × 10²³ = 1.204 × 10²⁴ ions

Tips

  • 1 mole of any substance contains Avogadro's number of particles
  • For molecular substances, particles = molecules
  • For ionic substances, particles = ions (total of all types)
  • For atomic substances, particles = atoms
