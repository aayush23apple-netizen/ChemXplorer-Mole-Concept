Moles to Volume Calculator

This calculator converts moles to volume for gases at STP using the molar volume: volume = moles × 22.4 L

How it works:

  1. Enter the number of moles of gas
  2. The calculator multiplies by the molar volume at STP
  3. Result shows the volume in liters
Volume (L) = moles × 22.4 L/mol
(at STP: 0°C and 1 atm)

Calculate Volume from Moles

Examples

  • Example 1: Calculate volume of 1 mole of any gas at STP
    volume = 1 × 22.4 = 22.4 liters
  • Example 2: Calculate volume of 0.5 moles of O₂ at STP
    volume = 0.5 × 22.4 = 11.2 liters
  • Example 3: Calculate volume of 2 moles of CO₂ at STP
    volume = 2 × 22.4 = 44.8 liters

Important Notes

  • This applies only to gases at STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure)
  • STP conditions: 0°C (273 K) and 1 atm pressure
  • All gases occupy 22.4 L per mole at STP (ideal gas behavior)
  • Not applicable to liquids or solids
